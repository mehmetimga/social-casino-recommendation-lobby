Title: Complete Guide to Progressive Jackpots
Source: Social Casino Knowledge Base
License: Internal

Progressive jackpots are the holy grail of casino gaming - massive prize pools that grow with every bet until one lucky player wins it all. Some progressives have created instant millionaires with a single spin.

How Progressive Jackpots Work:
A small percentage of every bet placed on a progressive game contributes to the jackpot pool. The jackpot continues growing until someone hits the winning combination, then resets to a seed amount and starts building again.

Contribution Rate:
Typically 1-5% of each bet goes to the jackpot. On a $1 bet with 3% contribution, $0.03 feeds the progressive. Multiply this by thousands of players across multiple casinos and jackpots grow quickly.

Types of Progressive Jackpots:

Standalone Progressives:
- Single machine with its own jackpot
- Jackpots typically $1,000 to $100,000
- Only bets on that machine contribute
- Hit more frequently than networked progressives
- Found in smaller casinos

Local/In-House Progressives:
- Multiple machines in one casino linked together
- Jackpots typically $10,000 to $1,000,000
- All linked machines contribute to same pool
- More players = faster growth
- Moderate hit frequency

Wide Area Network (WAN) Progressives:
- Machines across multiple casinos connected
- Jackpots can reach $10,000,000+
- Thousands of machines contributing
- Hit rarely but pay life-changing amounts
- Examples: Mega Moolah, Megabucks

Tiered Progressive Systems:
Many games offer multiple jackpot levels:

Mini Jackpot: Smallest, hits most often ($10-$100)
Minor Jackpot: Small but more frequent ($100-$1,000)
Major Jackpot: Significant, hits weekly/monthly ($10,000-$100,000)
Mega/Grand Jackpot: Life-changing, hits rarely ($1,000,000+)

How Jackpots Are Won:

Random Trigger:
Jackpot can hit on any spin regardless of symbols. Higher bets increase jackpot odds. Most modern progressives use this system.

Specific Combination:
Must land exact symbol combination (e.g., 5 jackpot symbols on payline). Rarer trigger method with bigger anticipation.

Bonus Game Trigger:
Enter bonus round, then win jackpot within that bonus. Mega Moolah and Mega Fortune use this approach.

Must-Hit-By Jackpots:
Jackpot must pay before reaching certain amount. Creates excitement as jackpot approaches threshold.

Important Considerations:

Lower Base RTP:
Progressive slots typically have lower RTP (88-94%) because portion goes to jackpot. Non-progressive versions of same game often have higher RTP.

Bet Requirements:
Some progressives require:
- Maximum bet for jackpot eligibility
- Minimum bet threshold
- All paylines active
Always check requirements before playing.

Jackpot Odds:
Mega jackpots have roughly 1 in 50 million odds - similar to lottery. Smaller tiers hit more reasonably often.

Volatility:
Most progressives are high volatility. Be prepared for dry spells between wins.

Strategy Tips:

1. Check Jackpot Size: Play when jackpot is well above average/seed amount
2. Verify Bet Requirements: Ensure you're eligible for all jackpot tiers
3. Budget Appropriately: High volatility needs larger bankroll
4. Don't Chase: Jackpot timing is random
5. Play for Fun: Treat jackpot as bonus, not expectation
6. Consider Smaller Progressives: Better odds, still exciting wins

When to Play Progressives:
- Jackpot significantly above seed/average
- You have bankroll for high volatility
- You want excitement of big win possibility
- Entertainment budget allows lower RTP

When to Avoid:
- Jackpot just paid out (near seed)
- Limited bankroll
- Seeking frequent wins
- Playing for steady RTP

The Mathematics:
As jackpot grows, theoretical RTP increases. At some point, progressive becomes positive expected value - but that point is usually astronomical and impractical to reach.

Perfect For:
- Dream chasers seeking life-changing wins
- Players who enjoy the excitement of growing jackpots
- Those with appropriate bankroll for high volatility
- Entertainment-focused players
